FRONT (Q)

What is the primary function of chlorophyll in photosynthesis?

BACK (A)

To absorb light energy (primarily blue and red wavelengths) to power the chemical reactions that convert CO2 and water into glucose.

FRONT (Q)

What are the two main stages of photosynthesis?

BACK (A)

1. Light-dependent reactions (in thylakoid membrane).
2. Calvin cycle / Light-independent reactions (in stroma).

FRONT (Q)

Where does photosynthesis occur?

BACK (A)

In the chloroplasts of plant cells.
